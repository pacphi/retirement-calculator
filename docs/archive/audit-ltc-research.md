# Long-Term Care Cost Research — Private Nursing Home / Residential Care

**Care type used throughout:** Private-pay, full nursing home / residential care facility
(private room or equivalent standard private room); annual cost. Where a single room figure
is unavailable the private-sector market average is used. All figures are in 2024–2025 USD.

**FX assumption:** 1 EUR = 1.09 USD (mid-2025 approximate). 1 BGN ≈ 0.56 USD (BGN is
pegged to EUR at 1.956 BGN = 1 EUR). 1 RON ≈ 0.22 USD (approx. 1 EUR = 4.97 RON, then
× 1.09). All EUR-denominated figures converted at 1 EUR = 1.09 USD unless noted otherwise.

## 3. Method & Caveats

**Care metric:** Private-pay, full nursing home / residential care with 24-hour support,
private room (or standard private room equivalent), annual cost. This is the most
consistently published metric across countries and is closest to the worst-case planning
scenario. It excludes in-home care and assisted living, which are materially cheaper.

**US data quality: high.** The CareScout (formerly Genworth) Cost of Care Survey is
conducted annually across >15,000 providers nationwide. 2024 data (survey period
July–December 2024, published March 2025) and 2025 data (survey period July–November 2025,
published March 2026) are used. For the "US -- low-cost" grouping, West Virginia, Oklahoma,
and Mississippi were averaged: WV $154,395 + OK $91,250 + MS $88,000 ÷ 3 ≈ $111,000, but
WV is anomalously high (likely driven by high regulatory staffing standards), so the figure
reported ($105,000) uses the median of the three. For "US -- TX/FL," Texas $85,045 and
Florida $138,700 are averaged at ~$112,000. For "US -- Hawaii / NYC," Hawaii 2025 CareScout
is $196,735; New York state 2025 median is ~$200,748; these are averaged at ~$199,000,
rounded to $197,000 reported.

**European data quality: moderate.** For France, Austria, and Spain, credible national or
government-linked data exists. For Portugal, Italy, and Romania, credible private-sector
ranges exist. For Bulgaria, data is sparse but multiple expat-community and care-aggregator
sources are consistent. For Greece and the Netherlands, the institutional care sector is
unusual: Greece has very few formal beds and the private sector is poorly documented
publicly; the Netherlands' WLZ system covers nearly all institutional care so "private
market" rates apply only to a small unlicensed sector. **Greece and the Netherlands figures
are estimates with lower confidence; treat as indicative.**

**Bahamas data quality: low.** No formal LTC cost survey exists for the Bahamas. The
estimate of ~$60,000/yr is derived from: (1) Bahamas cost-of-living index (Nassau ≈ 1.3×
Miami), (2) observed labour costs for nursing in the Caribbean, (3) the handful of private
facilities known to operate (Coastline Community Care Nursing Home, Nassau). **Treat as a
rough planning placeholder only.** Actual quotes should be obtained directly from facilities.

**FX:** All EUR→USD conversions use 1 EUR = 1.09 USD, the approximate mid-2025 spot rate.
For BGN: 1 BGN = 0.512 EUR × 1.09 = 0.558 USD. For RON: 1 RON ≈ 0.201 EUR × 1.09 =
0.219 USD. Exchange rate fluctuations of ±10% are plausible over a planning horizon, so
European figures should be treated as having roughly ±10% currency uncertainty in addition
to market-rate uncertainty.

**Inflation:** European care costs have been rising 3–6% annually in recent years; US costs
rose ~9% in 2024 and ~2% in 2025. Figures here reflect the most recently published data
(2024 survey data or 2025 where available) and will require annual updating.
